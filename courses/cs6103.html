Human-Centered AI

Human-Centered AI: From Learning Models to Responsible Systems (CS 6103)

Term: January 2025 - May 2025

Time: Wednesdays and Fridays (9:30-11:00 am)

Venue: IC3

Instructor: Arpit Agarwal

Outline: This is an advance course that introduces recent work in the field of Human-Centered AI where the goal is to design AI that aligns with human values and societal norms, operates in a safe and responsible manner.

Prerequisites: The class is intended for graduate students and senior undergraduates. We do not plan to impose any strict requisites that one should have completed to register for this course. However, students are expected to know the basics of linear algebra, probability, and calculus. Students should also have taken a basic machine learning course. Familiarity with Python and PyTorch would also be useful. There will be no pre-requisite quiz, we expect students to self-determine whether they are prepared to take this course or not.
